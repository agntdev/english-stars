# fix-492bdf6b3491ee23 — "Every other day" cron uses `*/2` day-of-month, firing on consecutive days at month boundaries

**Weight:** 0.0000 (share of project budget)
**Reward:** 0 ENSTAR

`buildCron()` in `src/reminder.ts:13-17` maps cadence `every_other_day` to `*/2` in the day-of-month field: `${minutes} ${hours} */2 * *`. Standard cron `*/2` means "every 2nd day starting from day 1 of each month" — odd-numbered calendar days (1,3,5,…). At month boundaries two odd-numbered days can be consecutive, e.g. Jan 31 → Feb 1, causing a reminder to fire on two back-to-back days, violating the "every other day" spec. A true every-48-hours schedule requires a different mechanism (e.g. BullMQ's `every` with millisecond intervals, or a stored last-fired timestamp).
